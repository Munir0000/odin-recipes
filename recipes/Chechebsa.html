Chechebsa

Description

Chechebsa, also known as kita firfir, is a traditional Ethiopian breakfast dish made from pieces of flatbread (kita) that are pan-fried with spiced butter (niter kibbeh) and berbere spice. It's a flavorful and hearty meal that is typically enjoyed with honey and yogurt on the side.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup water (adjust as needed)
  • 1/4 cup niter kibbeh (spiced clarified butter)
  • 1 tablespoon berbere spice mix
  • Honey, for serving (optional)
  • Yogurt, for serving (optional)

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, mix the flour and salt. Gradually add water and knead to form a smooth, pliable dough.
  2. Divide the dough into smaller portions and roll each portion into a thin flatbread (kita).
  3. Heat a dry skillet over medium heat and cook each flatbread for 2-3 minutes on each side, until lightly browned and cooked through. Remove from the skillet and let them cool slightly.
  4. Cut the flatbreads into bite-sized pieces.
  5. In the same skillet, melt the niter kibbeh over medium heat. Add the berbere spice mix and stir to combine.
  6. Add the pieces of flatbread to the skillet and toss to coat them evenly with the spiced butter.
  7. Cook for a few more minutes, stirring occasionally, until the flatbread pieces are crispy and well-coated with the spice mixture.
  8. Serve hot, optionally with honey and yogurt on the side.
